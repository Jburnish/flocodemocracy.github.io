Sternly Lettered Word Style Guide
  This guide defines the writing standards for all public materials produced for flocodemocracy.org. It ensures clarity, consistency, and a unified voice across statements, posts, testimony, and analysis. The style is direct, structured, and grounded in civic responsibility.

1. Purpose of the Style
  Provide a clear and authoritative voice for civic communication
  Maintain consistency across all written materials
  Support public trust through precision and transparency
  Ensure accessibility for readers with varied backgrounds

2. Core Principles
  Clarity
  Use plain language
  Avoid jargon unless it is defined
  Keep sentences focused and purposeful

Structure
  Use parallel formatting
  Present information in clean lists and sections
  Maintain logical flow from context to action

Tone
  Direct
  Firm when needed
  Respectful
  Grounded in community responsibility
  Formatting
  No em dashes
  Clean punctuation
  Consistent heading hierarch
  Markdown‑friendly structure

3. The Five Modes of the Sternly Lettered Word
  A. Civic Narrative
    Used for public explanations, community updates, and storytelling with purpose.

    Characteristics:
      Grounded in lived experience
      Connects individual actions to community outcomes
      Uses steady, confident pacing

  B. Testimony
    Used for public comment, hearings, and formal statements.

    Characteristics:
      Clear position
      Evidence‑based
      Respectful but firm
      Ends with a direct request or recommendation

C. Reflective
  Used for personal insight, leadership reflections, and community lessons.

  Characteristics:
    Calm and introspective
    Focused on learning and growth
    Connects past events to future direction

D. Strategic Analysis
  Used for planning, organizing, and decision‑making.

  Characteristics:
    Structured
    Data‑aware
    Identifies risks and opportunities
    Provides actionable steps

E. Systemic Threat Analysis
  Used when describing risks to democratic processes or community stability.

  Characteristics:
    Precise
    Evidence‑driven
    Avoids alarmism
    Focuses on mitigation and response

4. Formatting Standards
  Headings
    Use # through #### for clear hierarchy
    Keep titles short and functional

Lists
  Use bullet points for clarity
  Keep items parallel in structure
  Use numbered lists only when order matters

Paragraphs
  Keep paragraphs short
  One idea per paragraph

Links
  Use descriptive link text
  Avoid raw URLs in the body

Files
  Use lowercase filenames with hyphens
  Keep documents modular and easy to maintain

5. Voice Rules
  Speak with confidence, not aggression
  State facts plainly
  Avoid rhetorical questions
  Use active voice
  Keep emotional tone steady and grounded
  Prioritize community impact and shared responsibility

6. Examples
  Civic Narrative Example
    Our community has shown steady commitment to democratic participation. Each action, from attending meetings to supporting neighbors, strengthens the foundation we all rely on.

  Testimony Example
    I urge the commission to adopt the proposed zoning update. The data shows clear benefits for safety, accessibility, and long‑term growth. This decision will support residents and stabilize future development.

  Strategic Analysis Example
    The current outreach model is effective but limited. Expanding volunteer training and adding two new communication channels will increase reach and reduce bottlenecks.

7. When to Use This Guide
  Use this guide for:
    Website content
    Public statements
    Issue briefs
    Testimony
    Community updates
    Internal planning documents
    Any writing that represents the organization

8. Maintenance
  This guide should be reviewed quarterly. Updates should reflect:
    Lessons learned
    Community feedback
    Changes in organizational structure
    Evolving communication needs
