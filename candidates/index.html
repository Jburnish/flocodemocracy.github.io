Our Candidates

Florence County Democratic Candidates

Meet the Democrats running for office across Florence County — from city council and school board to the SC Governor and the U.S. Senate. Support them, share their stories, and help them win.

Candidate profiles

Lethonia "Peaches" Barnes

Mayor of Florence

Lethonia "Peaches" Barnes was elected Mayor of Florence, South Carolina in 2024, winning more than 60% of the primary vote. A graduate of South Carolina State University, Barnes owned and operated the largest beauty and barber salon in Florence and spent over 21 years as an administrator and instructor at Florence-Darlington Technical College. She previously served four years on Florence City Council before winning the mayoralty.

Mayor Barnes' priorities include community policing, economic development, downtown revitalization, and building a stronger, more unified Florence for every resident.

Chaquez T. McCall

Florence City Council, At-Large

Chaquez T. McCall made history in 2020 as the youngest person ever elected to the Florence City Council. A Florence native, McCall graduated from Francis Marion University and North Carolina Central University School of Law, where he focused on complex civil rights cases. He owns and operates McCall Law Firm in Florence.

Re-elected at-large in 2024, Councilman McCall chairs the Finance and Judicial Review Committees and is a tireless advocate for fiscal accountability, civil rights, and expanded opportunity for all Florence residents.

Website

Rev. Dr. Waymon Mumford

Florence County Council, District 7

Rev. Dr. Waymon Mumford has represented District 7 on the Florence County Council since 1994 and served as Council Chairman in 2019. A lifelong Florence resident, he spent 25 years with the Florence Police Department — retiring as Chief of Police — before transitioning to elected office. He holds degrees from Florence-Darlington Technical College, Limestone College, and the Interdenominational Theological Center, and earned an honorary Doctor of Laws from Morris College.

Dr. Mumford has served as pastor of Central Baptist Church since 1998. His decades of public service reflect a lifetime commitment to justice, public safety, and the well-being of Florence County residents.

Rev. Johnny R. Jenkins

Florence County Council, District 2

Rev. Johnny R. Jenkins is a lifelong Pamplico resident, community preacher, and former member of the Florence School District Two Board of Trustees. In 2024, Jenkins ran as the Democratic candidate for Florence County Council District 2, bringing attention to critical local issues including food security and essential services for lower Florence County communities.

Jenkins remains committed to his community, advocating for the elderly, rural families, and the residents of Pamplico and surrounding areas of lower Florence County.

Mitchell Kirby

Florence County Council, District 4

Mitchell Kirby served as a Florence County Council member representing District 4 for nearly 28 years before stepping aside. Based in Timmonsville, Kirby ran again in 2024, winning the Democratic primary and advancing to the general election — demonstrating the enduring trust his community has placed in his leadership.

Throughout his long tenure, Kirby worked to advance the interests of Florence County's rural communities and smaller municipalities, including Timmonsville and surrounding areas.

Rep. Terry Alexander

SC House of Representatives, District 59

Born and raised in Florence, Rep. Terry Alexander holds a Master of Divinity from Howard University and has served as a pastor, education consultant, and adjunct professor. He served on the Florence County Council for 16 years — twice as Chairman — before being elected to the SC House of Representatives in 2006.

In the legislature, Alexander serves as 2nd Vice Chair of the Education and Public Works Committee and has championed public education funding, voting rights, and economic development for Florence County families throughout his career.

Website

Rep. Roger K. Kirby

SC House of Representatives, District 101

Roger K. Kirby grew up in Lake City, South Carolina, and graduated from Furman University in 1982. A Realtor and certified appraiser by profession, Kirby was first elected to the SC House of Representatives in 2014 to serve the Lake City and lower Florence County area. He currently serves as the House Assistant Minority Leader and sits on the Labor, Commerce, and Industry Committee.

In 2025, Kirby was elected First Vice Chair of the South Carolina Democratic Party. He and his wife Pam have three children and are active in the Lake City community, where he has served as a music minister and in local civic organizations.

Rep. Jackie "Coach" Hayes

SC House of Representatives, District 55

Rep. Jackie E. "Coach" Hayes has represented a district that includes portions of Florence County in the SC House of Representatives since 1998. Born in Marion, SC, Hayes graduated from Catawba College and spent decades as an Athletic Director and football coach at Dillon High School, earning over 300 wins and Athletic Director of the Year honors.

In the legislature, Hayes serves on the influential House Ways and Means Committee and has made fully funding public education his top priority throughout his career, working tirelessly for students and families across the Pee Dee region.

Sen. Kent M. Williams

SC Senate, District 30

Senator Kent M. Williams has represented South Carolina Senate District 30 — which includes portions of Florence County — since 2004. Born in Marion and educated at Florence-Darlington Technical College and South Carolina State University, Williams has championed education funding, rural economic development, and support for Historically Black Colleges and Universities throughout his career.

He is widely recognized for securing record appropriations for SC State University and for his unwavering commitment to the communities of the Pee Dee region. Senator Williams serves on the Finance, Banking and Insurance, and Agriculture and Natural Resources committees.

Website

Sen. Ronnie A. Sabb

SC Senate, District 32

Senator Ronnie A. Sabb is an attorney who made history in 2014 as the first African American elected to represent SC Senate District 32. A graduate of Voorhees College and the University of Florida College of Law, Sabb served in the SC House of Representatives before moving to the Senate, where he has been re-elected twice — most recently in 2024.

Sabb championed the South Carolina Promise Scholarship, which provides free community college tuition to qualifying students, and has been a consistent voice for educational access and economic opportunity for residents across the Pee Dee region, including Florence County.
