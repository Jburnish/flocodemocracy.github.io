Our Candidates

Florence County Democratic Candidates

Meet the Democrats running for office across Florence County — from city council and school board to the SC Governor and the U.S. Senate. Support them, share their stories, and help them win.

Candidate profiles

Jermaine Johnson

Governor and Lieutenant Governor

Dr. Jermaine Johnson is a South Carolina state representative (District 52) and Democratic candidate for Governor in 2026. Born in Los Angeles on July 4, 1985, Johnson overcame hardship through basketball, earning a scholarship to the College of Charleston. He holds a bachelor's degree in communications from the College of Charleston, a master's in project management from Strayer University, and a doctorate in business administration from Northcentral University.

Johnson founded Dream Team Consulting and the New Economic Beginnings Foundation, a nonprofit helping people gain employment skills. A former adjunct professor at Midlands Technical College, he is running on a platform centered on strengthening public education, expanding healthcare access, fixing infrastructure, and creating good-paying jobs for South Carolinians.

Website

Mullins McLeod

Governor and Lieutenant Governor

Mullins McLeod is a third-generation attorney from Walterboro, South Carolina, with more than 25 years of experience in civil rights and personal injury litigation. His legal work includes serving as part of the team that negotiated an $88 million settlement for victims of the 2015 Mother Emanuel AME Church shooting in Charleston.

McLeod is running for Governor on a platform focused on fighting corruption, raising teacher pay, fixing roads and infrastructure, expanding affordable healthcare, and reforming ethics laws. He is self-funding his campaign to remain free from special interest influence.

Website

Vincent Coe

State Treasurer

Vincent Coe is a Florence, South Carolina banker and Democratic candidate for State Treasurer in 2026. A Darlington native whose father served in the Vietnam War, Coe brings more than 20 years of experience in banking to his campaign, serving as vice president at a regional bank. He is CRCM-certified (Certified Regulatory Compliance Manager) and serves as a FINRA arbitrator.

Coe is running on a platform of fiscal responsibility, transparency in state financial management, addressing the underfunded pension system, and ensuring every dollar of taxpayer money is used efficiently for the benefit of all South Carolinians.

Annie Andrews

U.S. Senate

Dr. Annie Andrews is a pediatrician and Democratic candidate for U.S. Senate in 2026, challenging incumbent Lindsey Graham. After earning her medical degree from the University of Cincinnati, she served for 15 years as a pediatric hospitalist at the Medical University of South Carolina. Her advocacy was galvanized by the 2018 Parkland school shooting, and she ran as the Democratic nominee for SC's 1st Congressional District in 2022.

Dr. Andrews is running on a platform of affordable healthcare, gun safety, quality public schools, and government accountability. She has pledged to accept no corporate PAC money. She lives in Mount Pleasant with her three children.

James E Jim Clyburn

U.S. House of Representatives, District 6

Congressman Jim Clyburn is the longest-serving member of South Carolina's congressional delegation, first elected in 1992 as the first African American to represent the state in Congress since 1897. He served as House Majority Whip and rose to become one of the most influential Democratic leaders in Congress, playing a pivotal role in the passage of the Affordable Care Act.

Clyburn is a champion for HBCUs, poverty alleviation through his 10-20-30 funding formula, rural broadband access, and voting rights. He is seeking an 18th term in the 2026 election to continue serving the communities of the 6th Congressional District, which includes Florence County.

Website

Frederick R Goodwin

U.S. House of Representatives, District 6

Frederick R. Goodwin is a Democratic candidate for U.S. House of Representatives in South Carolina's 6th Congressional District in the 2026 election. He is based in Santee, South Carolina, and is running to serve the communities of the 6th District, which includes Florence County.

John Gregory Vincent

U.S. House of Representatives, District 7

John Gregory Vincent is a retired U.S. Navy Command Master Chief with 20 years of service, including years in the submarine force. A resident of Myrtle Beach and small business owner, Vincent is a Democratic candidate for South Carolina's 7th Congressional District — which includes Florence County — in the 2026 election.

Running on a platform of bottom-up economics, protection of Medicaid and Medicare, expanded mental health resources, and commonsense gun safety, Vincent is working to become the first Democrat in over a century to represent the Grand Strand and Pee Dee in Congress.

Website

Jackie Hayes

SC House of Representatives, District 55

Rep. Jackie E. "Coach" Hayes has represented a district including portions of Florence County in the SC House of Representatives since 1998. Born in Marion, SC, Hayes graduated from Catawba College and spent decades as Athletic Director and head football coach at Dillon High School, earning over 300 wins and Athletic Director of the Year honors.

In the legislature, Hayes serves on the House Ways and Means Committee and has made fully funding public education his top priority, working tirelessly for students and families across the Pee Dee region.

Terry Alexander

SC House of Representatives, District 59

Rep. Terry Alexander is a Florence native who has served SC House District 59 — covering Florence and Marion counties — since 2006. He holds a Master of Divinity from Howard University and previously served on the Florence County Council for 16 years, twice as Chairman. He has also worked as a pastor, education consultant, and adjunct professor.

In the legislature, Alexander serves as 2nd Vice Chair of the Education and Public Works Committee and has championed public education funding, voting rights, and economic opportunity for the people of the Pee Dee.

Website

Adrian Peguese Carter

SC House of Representatives, District 60

Adrian Peguese Carter is a respected Florence attorney and Democratic candidate for SC House District 60 in the 2026 election. A member of the litigation team at Turner Padget Law, she was named the South Carolina Bar Young Lawyers Division's Young Lawyer of the Year in 2024, recognizing her exceptional contributions to the legal profession and her advocacy for underserved communities.

Carter is running to bring her legal expertise and commitment to community service to the SC House, where she aims to represent the residents of Florence County with dedication and integrity.

Melissa OShea

Robert Williams

SC House of Representatives, District 62

Rep. Robert Q. Williams has represented SC House District 62, covering parts of Darlington and Florence counties, since 2007. A veteran of the South Carolina Army National Guard who served in Iraq, Williams holds a B.S. from Voorhees College and an M.P.A. from Troy State University. He has worked as a Business Development Consultant and director of fatherhood engagement programs.

Known as "The People's Candidate," Williams champions equal rights, women's rights, fully funded public education, affordable healthcare, and commonsense gun safety. He serves on the Labor, Commerce, and Industry Committee.

Roger K Kirby

SC House of Representatives, District 101

Rep. Roger K. Kirby grew up in Lake City and is a Realtor and certified appraiser who has represented the Lake City and lower Florence County area in the SC House since 2014. He serves as House Assistant Minority Leader and sits on the Labor, Commerce, and Industry Committee. In 2025 he was elected First Vice Chair of the South Carolina Democratic Party.

Kirby and his wife Pam have three children and are deeply involved in the Lake City community. He is running for re-election in the redrawn District 101, which covers parts of Berkeley, Florence, and Williamsburg counties.

Susan McGill

Solicitor, Circuit 12 (Florence & Marion Counties)

Susan C. McGill is a Florence attorney and Democratic candidate for Solicitor of South Carolina's 12th Judicial Circuit, which serves Florence and Marion counties, in the 2026 election. McGill has served as an Assistant Solicitor in the 12th Circuit Solicitor's Office, where she has prosecuted cases in both Magistrates Court and General Sessions Court across a broad range of criminal matters.

Her legal practice also encompasses personal injury and family law. McGill brings hands-on courtroom experience and a commitment to justice for every resident of Florence and Marion counties.

Waymon Mumford

Florence County Council, District 7

Rev. Dr. Waymon Mumford has represented Florence County Council District 7 since 1994 and served as Council Chairman in 2019. A lifelong Florence resident, he spent 25 years with the Florence Police Department, retiring as Chief of Police, before transitioning to elected public service. He holds degrees from Florence-Darlington Technical College, Limestone College, and the Interdenominational Theological Center, and earned an honorary Doctor of Laws from Morris College.

Dr. Mumford has served as pastor of Central Baptist Church since 1998, and his decades of service reflect a lifetime commitment to justice, public safety, and the well-being of Florence County residents.
